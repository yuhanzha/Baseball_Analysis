Exploratory Data Analysis

Cluster Image



Above is an image of cluster analysis. As there are 13 types of different pitching method and 8 general types of pitcher, it is often quite confusing in expressing the differences among each type of pitchers. Each pitcher often concentrates in a few pitching methods, which further diversify the types of pitchers. To simplify the pitcher categories, unsupervised K-means clustering is performed in order to capture the similarity among the pitching methods. Elbow method is used to select the number of clusters, which is 3 in this case. By applying clustering analysis to more than 600 pitchers with their frequencies in use of each pitching methods, our model successful separates the pitcher into three major types. The composition of the pitching method of each pitcher can be found in the next figure.





Barchart - Pitcher Type by Clusters



Above is a barchart of pitcher types based on the result of clustering analysis. As shown, clustering analysis determines three major types of pitchers. Cluster 0 consists the most diverse pitchers such that they concentrate in various pitching type including FF – four seam fastball, FT – two seam fastball, and FC – cutter fastball. The pitchers in cluster 1 are less diversify as most of them concentrate in CH – changeup along with some concentrations of SL – slider. Pitchers in cluster 2 is the least diverse with a high concentration in FF – fastball.
